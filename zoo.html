Welcome

Explore wildlife, learn about conservation, and plan your visit

Featured Exhibits

Trophical Rainforest

Step into a humid, living rainforest filled with birds, amphibians, reptiles, and tropical plants. Interactive displays teach conservation of tropical ecosystems.

Savannah Plains

Wide open habitats showcase giraffes, zebras and mixed-species exhibits that model African grassland communities and their conservation needs.

Living Northern Trail

The “Living Northwest Trail” is designed to evoke cold-climate habitats: forested taiga, mountainous / montane zones, streams and lakes, tundra-style open areas, rocky cliffs (for mountain goats), and river or aquatic habitats for species like otters and bears.

Temperate Forest

Our Temperate Forest zone features red pandas, Asian cranes and maned wolves in the Wildlife Survival Zone. Rather than grouping animals just by geography, the zoo uses a “bioclimatic zone” approach: animals are grouped by the type of habitat they’re adapted to.

Animals

Malayan Tiger Panthera tigris jacksoni

A critically endangered tiger subspecies native to Peninsular Malaysia. Conservation programs focus on anti-poaching and habitat protection.

Giant Forest Tortoise Aldabrachelys gigantea

A great longevity and important ecological roles like seed dispersal, believed to be among the most primitive of living tortoises, based on molecular and morphological

African Lion Panthera leo

Iconic savannah predator important for maintaining ecosystem balance. Lions are social cats living in prides, and are a vulnerable species due to habitat loss and human-wildlife conflict. The lion is the national animal of several African countries and is a symbol of strength and courage.

Humboldt Penguins
Spheniscus humboldti

A vulnerable coastal species depending on cold, nutrient-rich currents are good indicators of ocean health. Humboldt penguins are medium-sized, black-and-white seabirds native to the coasts of Peru and Chile, and are threatened by habitat loss and climate change.

Snow Leopard Panthera uncia

An alpine specialist whose conservation helps protect fragile high-elevation ecosystems. Snow leopards are elusive big cats adapted to cold, mountainous habitats are vulnerable due to poaching and habitat loss.

Assam Rhino
Rhinoceros unicornis

This rhino is a herbivore, eating grasses, leaves, and fruits, and is mostly solitary. It is found in the northeastern states of India and is a vulnerable species due to habitat loss and poaching.

Plains Zebra Equus quagga

The Plains Zebra is the most common and widespread species of zebra. It is known for its striking black-and-white stripes, which are unique to each individual, just like human fingerprints.

Giraffe
Giraffa camelopardalis

The giraffe is the tallest land animal, with a very long neck to reach leaves on tall trees. The species is known for its distinctive spotted coat and long legs.

Jaguar Panthera onca

Its distinctively marked coat features pale yellow to tan coloured fur covered by spots that transition to rosettes on the sides, although a melanistic black coat appears in some individuals.

Patas-Monkey
Erythrocebus baumstarki

A critically endangered Monkey subspecies native to Patas-Monkey. Conservation programs focus on anti-poaching and habitat protection.

Brown Bear
Ursus arctos

The fur ranges in color from cream to reddish to dark brown. Of the land carnivorans, it is rivaled in size only by its closest relative, the polar bear, which is much less variable in size and slightly bigger average.

Maned Wolf Chrysocyon brachyurus

The maned wolf's footprints are similar to those of the dog, but have disproportionately small plantar pads when compared to the well-opened digit marks. The maned wolf is the only species in the genus Chrysocyon.
